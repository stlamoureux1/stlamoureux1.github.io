Current Pursuits

I’m a later-in-life student focused on applied math and high-performance computing. This semester I am working with Greg Parker, a post-doctoral fellow at UC Berkeley, on next-generation techniques for computer simulation of magnetic confinement fusion reactors. As part of this I’m doing a lot of background work learning functional analysis, differential geometry, multilinear algebra, and some statistical mechanics.

In summer 2025, I interned at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in the Center for Computational Science and Engineering, where I began working in high-performance computing and numerical analysis, as well as applying AI techniques to thermodynamic problems in fluid simulations.

My recent course work has included copmuter architectures, data structures & algorithms, real analysis, abstract algebra, and partial differential equations.

Background

I originally earned my bachelor’s and master’s degrees in linguistics at UC Berkeley. My initial interest in computer science emerged in that time through the applications of substructural logics / type theories to natural language syntax and semantics. This lead me to functional programming, formal semantics, and the Curry-Howard correspondence.

After my studies I worked in legal tech, doing information retrieval, background research, and electronic discovery at H5 (now Lighthouse Document Technologies) in San Francisco. I participated there on a strategy team for advancing our machine learning stack (in the pre-LLM era). Life events took me away from the professional world, and I decided to refocus my efforts on a more technical career trajectory.
